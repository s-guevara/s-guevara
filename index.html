A VISUAL SUMMARY & ANALYSIS OF TERRORISM

A Decade of Activity Around the World & In The US

Overview

On April 13, 2021, the Biden Administration announced the full withdrawal of US forces from Afghanistan on September 11th, two decades after the most infamous terrorist attack on US soil, and two decades after the United States declared a war on terror. In light of this and in light of the perceived rise in domestic extremism since the end of the Obama Administration, this project aims to take a brief, macro view of the trends of terrorist activity, both across the globe and in the United States. The data on global terrorism used for this project comes from the Global Terroism Database while the data for the United States comes from The Washington Post/CSIS.



Global

We start with a look at terrorism throughout the world over the last four decades...

1980 - 2019


There have been significantly more fatalities due to terrorist activity across the world in the last decade compared to the previous 3 decades. Iraq, Afghanistan and Nigeria have seen the most deaths at the hands of terrorists groups. In the last couple of years, howeover, it seems there's been a downward trend in these fatalities across the most active countries.



2009-2019


The majority of attacks throughout the world in the past decade largely occured in Africa, the Middle East, and Southern Asia. The most activity recorded on the eastern side of the Pacific occured in Columbia.

Looking at the data differently, in the past 10 years, the countries that had the most casualties were low income and lower middle income countries. Iraq notably had the largest number of casualties among the upper middle income group, and the United States and France each had the most among the high income countries. In general, high income countries appear to have the least number of casualties.

Also among the most dangerous countries are Pakistan, Yemen, Somalia, India, the Philippines, and Turkey.

Baghadad had the deadliest terrorist activity among all cities with 37% of all deaths in the last decade. Some of the largest cities in Pakistan and Iraq also had the most deaths: Karachi, Quetta and Peshawar in Pakistan; Mosul, Kirk and Baqubah, aside form Baghdad, in Iraq.

The Taliban and the Islamic State of Iraq and the Levant (ISIL) where the most active groups in South Asia and the Middle East regions, respectively. Al-Shabaab and Boko Haram led the greatest number of attacks in the Sub-Saharan Africa region, while the New People's Army lead the most attacks in Southeast Asia.

The Type of Extremism Most Prominent

In truth, all groups included in the global data are considered extremist groups considering that their beliefs take them so far as to cause violence. However, for this next visual, the perpetrators that were labeled "extremists" but had no affiliation to a specific group were separated and recategorized into predominantly four groups: governement extremists, religious extremists, ethnic extremists, and nationalist extremists. "Anti-" groups were placed in the same categories as their pro- counter parts. For example, "Anti-Christian" would be in the religious extremist group type, just as "Christian extremists" would be.
The most active type of extremists, thus, are those driven by their religious beliefs, followed by ethnic extremists. But as the chart shows, religious extremists claim just a bit more than three fourths of all terrorist activity in the world.

If we look at the type of extremists predominant across income levels, religious groups remain the most active, though in lower middle income countries ethnic extremism is the most dominant.
Among high income countries, notably the United States is among the top two countries with the most extremists in almost every category. If you select the category of extremism on the chart above, you can see the subcategory of groups that make up that category. For example, in selecting "religious" we see that Houthi extremism is the most active type of religious group in Saudia Arabia, and Anti-Muslim extremists are the most active in the United States.

Though several common targets, the most common victim is the unnamed civilian. In the almost 9,000 attacks throughout the last decade, over 22,000 lives were lost as a result. However, many of the other victims tended to be in the military, law enforcement, or governement.
Schools and places of worship also tended to be common targets for terrorist groups across the globe.

The preferred weapon, and the deadliest weapon, used were bombs/explosives by about 50%. Armed assualts were also the very popular. Combined, these two weapons resulted in over 400,000 documented casualties.
