Terror in the US, 2009-2021

Orlando, Florida had the greatest number of deaths over the last decade with 49 lives lost to the terrorist attack on June 12, 2016 at the Pulse nightclub shooting.
The second deadliest attack occured in El Paso, Texas on August 3, 2019.


California and New York were the most prone to terrorism, which makes sense as these are states with some of the most dense cities in the country.

Unsurprisingly, then, New York City has had the most incidences among all other cities. D.C. follows. Arlington, VA, albiet not as dense as many of the other cities on the list, is among the least in the top 15 cities, likely due to its proximity to the capital.

Observing fatalities through the decade, 2016 was the deadliest year in the United States as a results of extremism, though 2020 in fact had the most attacks. This is doubtlessly correlated to the exogenous shocks of 2020, such as the pandemic, presidential election, and the unrest over police brutality...

And we can see that. In 2020, the majority of attacks were indeed targeted at governemnt and police as well as demonstrators. While private individuals tend to be common targets over the years, it relatively constituted a low portion of attacks in 2020. Religious institutions also saw the least attacks relative to recent years.


Among all type of targets, individuals were indeed the most common victims.

The plot above and the chart below both show frequent victims of terrorists in more detail. As we can see, Black and African American individuals have been the most targeted individuals.
Muslim individuals have been the second most.

As we would expect, using the chart below, we can see that white supremacists have been the greatest perpetrators of these attacks. In fact, when selecting white supremacists, the data shows us that they can actually been the most aggressive to various groups of victims -- incuding other white individuals.

So where do these group of victims more often get attacked?

Adding a layer to show Black and African American populations across the country, we see that there is a correlation between attacks and Black populations. This layer can also be used as a proxy for population density of hispanic and asian minority groups as well. States with more minority groups are states with more diversity and greater general population, therefore probability suggests these states would be the most suseptible to attacks. Nonetheless, this map does support the findings from the previous charts, that Black communities are the most victimized groups in the US.

Just as in the global scale, terrorist groups in the United States tend to prefer the use of explosives, as well as armed attacks. In fact, according to the chart, explosives have been used more than all other weapons except firearms combined.

And finally, the data shows that the most common form of social media these terrorist groups used to communicate were Facebook, encrypted chats, instagram, and youtube. However, they mainly used a variety of these channels to do so.
